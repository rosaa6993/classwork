The Others in Art considers itself to be a place that fuels diverse creativity, ignites the minds, and provides inspiration for those seeking a profession in arts, or simply to inspire. The Others in Art is dedicated to fostering a conversation between the past and the present, the established and the experimental. Our goal is to give exposure to voices not often heard or seen in the arts. The Others in Art was founded in 2016 as an educational web gallery by students at the Borough of Manhattan Community College. Central to its mission is to encourage an ever deeper understanding and enjoyment of art of the past and the present created by a diverse local, national and international audience that it serves and represents. We hope to foster and continue to be there for the people who love art and design, create it and tinker with it. Art for the people by the people.

Ai Wei Wei

Ai Weiwei is an artist. Born in 1957, he currently resides and works in Beijing.

Ai Weiwei is one of Chinas most prolific and provocative contemporary artists. Featuring over forty works spanning more than twenty years, Ai Weiwei: According to What? explores universal topics of culture, history, politics, and tradition, showcasing the artist’s remarkably interdisciplinary career as a photographer, sculptor, architect, and activist.

Tree 2010 is a monumental sculpture assembled from the dry, dead branches, roots and trunks of numerous species of tree, such as camphor, cedar and ginkgo, that Ai Weiwei gathered from across the mountainous southern region of his native China.

Stephen Berks

Stephen Burks was born in 1969 and raised in Chicago, Illinois.

Stephen Burks was born in 1969 and raised in Chicago, Illinois. Burks studied architecture at the Illinois Institute of Technology, and product design at Institute of Design, in Chicago. He then studied architecture at Columbia University's Graduate School of Architecture and Planning in New York.

Burks became the first African American to win the National Design Award for product design in 2015. He curated an exhibition at New Yorks Museum of Arts and Design and was featured in a solo exhibition at the Studio Museum farther uptown in Harlem.

Ana-mendieta

An influential artist best known for her "earth-body" performances.

An influential artist best known for her “earth-body” performances, as she called them, Ana Mendieta explored her identity as a female emigrant in work that also encompassed photography, film, and sculpture. Exiled from Cuba at the age of 12 and sent to an orphanage in Iowa, Mendieta used the earth as a site to address issues of displacement, impressing her body in various outdoor locations and recording its imprint in photographs and video.

In a relatively short span of time between 1972 and 1985, Ana Mendieta created one of the richest and most distinct bodies of work among the early earthworks artists. Though she was of the generation of artists like Robert Smithson and Michael Heizer, she eschewed their geometric, formal minimalism in favor of a fully embodied, spiritually infused earth art.
